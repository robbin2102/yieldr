The Problem

Why earning and scaling alpha in DeFi is harder than it should be.

For Investors

You want to earn alpha by following top DeFi traders. But you face two problems:

üîç Discovery Problem

Finding top traders across DeFi protocols is fragmented and manual. There's no unified view of who's actually generating alpha across Avantis, Hyperliquid, Polymarket, Kalshi, Aerodrome, Uniswap, and other protocols.

‚Üí Solved by: AI agents discover and rank top traders across protocols MVP

ü§ù Trust Problem

Copy trading platforms exist, but there's no way to validate the performance they claim. Even when wallet addresses are provided, there's massive friction to pull transaction data and do true analysis. Without understanding what actually drives a trader's alpha, investing is blind guesswork.

‚Üí Solved by: AI analyzes on-chain transactions, explains alpha drivers MVP

For Traders

Traders who generate consistent alpha have no way to scale:

‚úì Validation Problem

No place to publicly validate performance in a verifiable way. No path from managing $200K personal capital to $2M+ with outside investors. You're stuck trading your own capital with no way to prove your edge to potential investors.

‚Üí Solved by: AI agent-driven onchain performance validation + Fund management Beta v1.0

üîí Edge Preservation Problem

Top traders don't want their wallet transactions parsed and strategies reverse-engineered. No incentive structure exists for them to let their alpha be accessible to investors. Why would they share their edge for free?

‚Üí Solved by: Transparency levels (performance-only vs full) + Compensation for alpha v1.0

DeFi Vaults Limited ‚ö† Protocol-specific ‚úó None ‚ö† Basic fees only

The Two Missing Layers

Intelligence Layer: Even if all wallet data were indexed, alpha isn't constant. Traders have hot and cold streaks. Markets change. You'd need to constantly monitor and rotate capital ‚Äî a full-time job. AI agents solve this.

Trust Layer: Even with intelligence, there's no way to execute trustlessly. Smart contracts with coded risk limits, transparency options, and compensation structures solve this. This is what enables traders to participate without giving away their edge for free.

FAQ

Common questions about Yieldr.

General

What is Yieldr?

Yieldr is an AI-enabled decentralized asset management platform. It's a two-sided marketplace where investors discover top traders & fund managers while traders validate performance and raise capital ‚Äî all powered by AI agents and secured by smart contracts.

What can I do with Yieldr today?

In Phase 1 (MVP), you can use AI agents to discover top DeFi traders, analyze their performance, get recommendations on your positions, and monitor your portfolio with proactive alerts. Think of it as a smart copilot for DeFi investing and trading.

Is Yieldr custodial?

No. In Phase 1, your agent analyzes on-chain data but doesn't execute trades. In Phase 3 (v1.0), when the Trust Layer launches, all capital deployment will be non-custodial via smart contracts ‚Äî you maintain full custody of your funds.

Which chains and protocols does Yieldr support?

Currently: Base chain only, covering Avantis (perps), Aerodrome (DEX), Uniswap v3, and other major Base protocols. We'll expand to other chains in later phases based on community demand.

For Investors

How does Yieldr help me find top traders?

Your AI agent scans on-chain transaction data across DeFi protocols to identify wallets with strong, verifiable performance. It ranks traders by metrics like win rate, risk-adjusted returns, max drawdown, and consistency ‚Äî all based on real, unfakeable on-chain data.

Can I copy trade top traders right now?

Not yet. Phase 1 (MVP) provides intelligence ‚Äî discovery, analysis, and recommendations. Phase 3 (v1.0) will enable trustless capital deployment via smart contracts, allowing you to follow traders' strategies automatically.

How do I know the performance data is real?

All data comes directly from on-chain transactions. It can't be faked or manipulated. Your agent parses wallet transactions from protocols like Avantis and Aerodrome to calculate performance metrics in real-time.

For Traders

How can Yieldr help me raise capital?

Yieldr gives you a path from solo trader to fund manager. Build a verified track record in Phase 1. Train your execution agent in Phase 2 (Beta). Launch your fund and raise capital in Phase 3 (v1.0) via smart contracts that let investors deploy trustlessly while you earn performance fees.

Will my trading strategies be public?

You control transparency. In v1.0, you'll choose between performance-only mode (investors see results but not individual trades) or full disclosure mode (complete transparency). You get compensated for alpha either way ‚Äî so sharing your edge isn't free.

What fees do traders earn?

In v1.0, traders can set performance fees (typically 10-20% of profits) when raising capital. You also earn a share of platform revenue if you're a top-performing fund manager. Fee structures are coded into smart contracts and enforced automatically.

Token & Economics

When is the $YLDR token launching?

Token Generation Event (TGE) is planned for Q1 2025. Until then, you can earn points by using the platform, which will convert to $YLDR allocation at TGE.

Do I need $YLDR to use Yieldr?

No. Basic agent features (discovery, analysis) are free. Staking $YLDR unlocks advanced features like fine-tuned agents, proactive monitoring, and fee discounts ‚Äî but the core product works without tokens.

How do I earn $YLDR before TGE?

Use the platform daily, analyze traders, build track records (if you're a trader), refer friends, and contribute to the community (Discord, bug reports, feature ideas). All activity earns points that convert to tokens at TGE.

AI & Privacy

What data does my AI agent access?

Your agent reads public on-chain data (transactions, positions) from your connected wallet and other wallets you ask it to analyze. It does NOT access private keys, cannot execute transactions without your approval, and only reads publicly available blockchain data.

Can my agent execute trades without my permission?

In Phase 1 (MVP): No. Your agent only provides recommendations. In Phase 3 (v1.0): Only if you explicitly enable semi-autonomous mode and set coded risk limits in the smart contract. You maintain full control and can revoke permissions anytime.

Is my chat history with the agent private?

Yes. Your conversations are private and encrypted. We use them to personalize your agent's recommendations, but they're never shared publicly or sold to third parties.

Glossary ‚Üí

Glossary

Key terms and definitions.

AI Agent

An autonomous or semi-autonomous AI system that can perform tasks on behalf of a user. In Yieldr, your AI agent discovers traders, analyzes performance, recommends actions, and (in later phases) can execute trades based on your preferences and risk limits.

Alpha

Excess returns generated by a trading strategy or fund manager, beyond what would be expected from market movements alone. In DeFi, alpha typically refers to profit generated through skill (strategy, timing, selection) rather than luck or broad market growth.

AUM (Assets Under Management)

The total market value of assets that a fund manager or trader is managing on behalf of investors. Higher AUM typically indicates stronger investor confidence and track record.

Base

An Ethereum Layer 2 blockchain developed by Coinbase. Yieldr is initially built on Base, covering protocols like Avantis, Aerodrome, and Uniswap v3 on Base.

Fine-Tuning

The process of training an AI model on specific data to specialize it for a particular task. In Yieldr, agents are fine-tuned on top traders' strategies AND your personal preferences to become personalized investment copilots.

Fund Manager

A trader who manages capital on behalf of outside investors, typically earning a management fee and/or performance fee. Yieldr enables traders to transition from solo trading to fund management via verified track records and smart contract infrastructure.

IL (Impermanent Loss)

The opportunity cost of providing liquidity to an AMM pool vs. simply holding the assets. Occurs when the price ratio of pooled assets changes. Yieldr agents help LPs monitor IL and optimize yield.

Intelligence Layer

Yieldr's AI-powered discovery, analysis, and recommendation system. The Intelligence Layer is live in Phase 1 (MVP) and enables users to find top traders, understand alpha drivers, and get data-backed investment advice.

LP (Liquidity Provider)

A user who deposits token pairs into a decentralized exchange pool (like Uniswap or Aerodrome) to enable trading, earning fees in return. LPs face risks like impermanent loss and need to actively manage positions for optimal returns.

Max Drawdown

The largest peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value over a specified period. A key risk metric ‚Äî a trader with 80% win rate but 50% max drawdown is riskier than one with 60% win rate and 15% max drawdown.

Non-Custodial

A system where users retain full control of their funds and private keys. Yieldr's Trust Layer (v1.0) will be non-custodial ‚Äî capital is deployed via smart contracts, not transferred to Yieldr or fund managers.

On-Chain

Data or transactions recorded on a public blockchain, making them transparent, verifiable, and immutable. Yieldr analyzes on-chain transaction data to validate trader performance ‚Äî it can't be faked.

Perps (Perpetual Futures)

Derivative contracts that allow traders to speculate on asset prices with leverage, without an expiration date. Popular DeFi perp platforms include Avantis, Hyperliquid, and GMX.

PM (Portfolio Manager) Agent

An AI agent specialized in portfolio management for investors. In Yieldr Beta, PM agents are fine-tuned on top traders' strategies and your specific risk tolerance & goals to provide personalized investment advice.

Risk-Adjusted Returns

Returns measured relative to the risk taken to achieve them. A trader with 50% annual return and 10% max drawdown has better risk-adjusted returns than one with 60% return and 40% drawdown. Metrics like Sharpe Ratio measure this.

Sharpe Ratio

A measure of risk-adjusted returns, calculated as (return - risk-free rate) / volatility. Higher Sharpe = better return for the risk taken. A Sharpe >1.0 is good, >2.0 is excellent.

Smart Contract

Self-executing code on a blockchain that automatically enforces agreement terms without intermediaries. Yieldr's Trust Layer uses smart contracts to enable non-custodial fund management with coded risk controls.

Staking
